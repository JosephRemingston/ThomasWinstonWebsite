What Is a Hernia?

A hernia occurs when tissue or part of an organ pushes through a weak spot in the abdominal wall.

Common Types

  • Inguinal: Most frequent, especially in men
  • Femoral: Lower groin area
  • Umbilical: Near the belly button
  • Incisional: Through a previous surgical scar
  • Epigastric & Hiatal: Less common

Symptoms & When to Seek Help

  • A visible or reducible bulge
  • Aching or discomfort on standing, lifting or straining
  • Sudden severe pain, irreducibility, or redness: Seek immediate medical attention

Hernia Treatment Options

Watchful Waiting

Suitable for small, symptom-free hernias. Regular monitoring is essential.

Surgical Repair

  • Open Mesh Repair: Traditional method with local/general anaesthesia
  • Laparoscopic Repair (TEP/TAPP): Keyhole surgery, faster recovery
  • Complex Repairs: For recurrent, large, or strangulated hernias

Why Laparoscopic Repair?

  • Smaller incisions, fewer infections
  • Faster return to normal activity
  • Lower risk of chronic pain

What to Expect – Procedure & Recovery

Before Surgery

  • Examination, blood tests, imaging if needed

During Surgery

  • Usually a 1–2 hour day-case with mesh placement via keyhole

Post-Op Recovery

  • Mobilise early
  • Light activity in a few days
  • Full recovery: 4–6 weeks
  • Pain managed with paracetamol/NSAIDs

FAQs Related to Hernia

Is mesh always used?

Yes, it strengthens the repair and lowers recurrence. Biologic mesh is used in some cases.

When can I drive or return to work?

Light driving in a few days; full return in 4–6 weeks.

What if the hernia comes back?

Reconsult if symptoms or bulge return. Options available.

Can I wait and see?

Yes, for small painless hernias. But monitoring is critical.
